Energy of the Future â€“ Solar Energy

Globally increasing energy consumption coupled with rising environmental concerns such as greenhouse gas emission is expected to drive renewable energy market over the next few years. Now it is the need of time. Amongst renewable energy resources, solar energy is the most reliable and clean source of energy. Over a past few years there has been a remarkable research and development in increasing the efficiency of solar panels. The cost has also been reduced drastically which eventually increased the demand of the solar energy worldwide. Thus governments are planning to inculcate a large sector of energy consumption through solar and wind energy. Power companies and government provide subsidy on solar power energy generation in numerous regions such as China, Japan, India, Germany, and Canada. Europe is expected to emerge as the leading regional market and is expected to account for more than 30% of the total market by 2022. Germany is one of the major countries utilizing solar energy to optimum levels. Solar power in India is a fast developing industry. The country's solar installed capacity reached 25.21 GW as of 31 December 2018. India has set a new target of achieving 100 GW of solar power by 2022. Many start-ups are evolving at local as well as global platform to meet the energy demand. It is gaining wide acceptance globally owing to reasons such as low operation, maintenance cost and early payback period. Thus it can be concluded that solar energy is the energy of future.
